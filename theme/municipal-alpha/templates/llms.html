A knowledge graph of US local government built from daily collection of agendas, minutes, check registers, permits, RFPs, capital plans, and assessor records across 3,400+ of the ~19,500 US municipalities. Municipal Alpha shows you what a town is working toward before it decides, before the vote, before the permit, before the RFP. It reads the precursor chain (the working-group discussion, the draft moratorium, the early agenda item) in 3,400+ of the ~19,500 US municipalities, months before the decision is on record. Buyers use it to act on the front end of the timeline instead of the finish line: to compete on an RFP before it becomes an RFP, to trade on a municipal signal before the building permit, to flag credit or infrastructure risk before it becomes a rating action or a claim. Documents are classified by type, vendor names are resolved to public-company tickers, and the signal chain is traced from the earliest trigger to the eventual decision.
