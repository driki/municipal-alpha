The Falmouth Town Council voted 6-1 Monday to adopt a new short-term rental registration ordinance. The ordinance requires property owners offering rentals of fewer than 30 days to register with the town clerk and maintain a local contact person. The council had debated the measure across four sessions since January.

Councilor Amy Kuhn, who cast the dissenting vote, said she supported registration but felt the proposed fee structure was too low to cover enforcement costs. The ordinance takes effect May 1.

The Conservation Commission reviewed a proposed trail easement along the Presumpscot River corridor Wednesday. The easement would connect the existing Presumpscot River Trail to a new trailhead on Allen Avenue Extension, adding approximately 1.2 miles of publicly accessible riverfront path. The Falmouth Land Trust is negotiating terms with the landowner. The commission voted to recommend the easement to the Town Council pending a boundary survey.

The Energy Committee presented its municipal solar feasibility study to the Town Council as an informational item. The study, conducted with technical assistance from Efficiency Maine, identified three town-owned parcels as potentially suitable for solar installations totaling 800 kW. The committee estimated annual savings of $95,000 to $120,000 in electricity costs. No action was taken; the council asked for a follow-up presentation comparing ownership versus power purchase agreement models.

The Recycling Committee discussed the town's food waste composting pilot, which began in January. Participation has reached 340 households, ahead of the 250-household target. The committee will recommend expanding the program to all residential accounts in the FY2027 budget request.

- A fence permit at 7 Depot Road
