*Pocatello, Idaho (pop. 56,000) -- Bannock County. Week of March 15-21, 2026.*

---

The Pocatello City Council approved a $4.2 million upgrade to the city's water pollution control facility at its Thursday meeting. The project covers new ultraviolet disinfection equipment and aeration system improvements to meet tightened Idaho DEQ discharge limits. The council awarded the engineering contract to Stantec, which designed the facility's original expansion in 2019. Construction is expected to begin in fall 2026 and take 14 months.

Council Member Roger Bray asked about the funding source. Public Works Director Hannah Seevers said the project will be funded through a combination of State Revolving Fund loans and existing water/sewer enterprise fund reserves, with no impact on the general fund.

The Airport Commission met Wednesday to review bids for the terminal building expansion project at the Pocatello Regional Airport. Three general contractors submitted proposals, ranging from $6.1 million to $7.8 million. Airport Manager Dave Gibbons said the FAA has committed $4.5 million in Airport Improvement Program funding, with the remainder covered by a state grant and local match. The commission recommended the low bidder, McAlvain Construction, and forwarded the recommendation to the City Council for approval.

The Planning and Zoning Commission held a public hearing Tuesday on a proposed 84-lot residential subdivision north of Satterfield Drive. The developer, Rockwell Homes, presented a plan for a mix of single-family and townhouse lots with a community park and trail connection to the Portneuf Greenway. Several residents from the adjacent Northgate neighborhood raised concerns about traffic on Hawthorne Road. The commission continued the hearing and asked the developer to provide a traffic impact study.

The Youth Advisory Board discussed plans for a summer jobs fair in partnership with Idaho State University's career services office. The board approved a budget of $1,200 for venue and printing costs, funded by the city's youth programs line item.

## Permits Issued

Nine building permits were issued this week:

- Two new single-family homes in the Chubbuck Crossing development
- A commercial tenant buildout for a physical therapy clinic on Yellowstone Avenue
- A residential addition at 845 North Garfield Avenue
- Three residential re-roofing permits
- A sign permit for a Stinker Stores location on Fifth Avenue
- A residential solar installation at 1120 South Second Avenue
