## City Government

Pocatello's City Council engaged in a busy week of meetings, culminating in a regular session that saw significant discussion on the city's housing strategy. On Monday, March 16, a Special City Council Meeting was held at 4:00 PM, followed by a City Council Special Clarification Meeting at 5:30 PM. These sessions focused primarily on a proposed amendment to the city's zoning ordinance regarding residential density, a topic that has seen increasing attention across the region. City Planner Sarah Jenkins presented a staff report outlining potential impacts of the changes, particularly concerning areas near Idaho State University. The Council reviewed a comprehensive packet of materials related to the proposal, engaging in detailed questions with staff and legal counsel. Special Clarification Meeting Agenda

The regular City Council Meeting, held at 6:00 PM on Monday, March 16, opened with a public hearing on the aforementioned zoning amendment. Several residents provided testimony, both in favor of increasing housing options and expressing concerns about infrastructure strain. Councilwoman Maria Rodriguez emphasized the need for balanced growth, citing a recent MunicipalAlpha signal indicating high regulatory contagion around housing density. Following deliberation, the Council voted 4-2 to approve the first reading of the ordinance amendment, directing staff to incorporate minor language adjustments before a second reading. Regular City Council Meeting Agenda

Throughout the week, several of Pocatello's 13 advisory committees convened. The Pocatello Mayor's Youth Advisory Council met on Monday, March 16, to discuss upcoming community service projects, including a proposed clean-up along the Portneuf Greenway trail system. On Tuesday, March 17, the Pocatello America250 Ad Hoc Committee held a meeting to continue planning for the nation's semiquincentennial celebrations in 2026, exploring partnerships with local historical societies. Later that day, the Library Board discussed new acquisitions and summer reading program initiatives, while the CDBG Advisory Committee reviewed applications for community development block grants, prioritizing projects addressing affordable housing and infrastructure improvements. Pocatello Mayor's Youth Advisory Council Agenda

Wednesday, March 18, saw a Special Meeting of the Portneuf Valley Environmental Fair Committee, which finalized vendor applications and programming for the annual fair, with a focus on educational outreach regarding the FMC phosphorus plant Superfund site remediation efforts. Concurrently, the Housing Alliance & Community Partnerships met to strategize on increasing affordable housing options within the city, a direct response to the community's growing needs and the City Council's ongoing discussions. ## Schools

The Pocatello/Chubbuck School District 25 Board of Trustees did not hold a scheduled meeting this week. However, district administration continued preparations for the upcoming spring break. Superintendent Dr. Emily Chen announced that enrollment figures remained stable across the district's 20 schools, with particular growth noted at Highland High School. Facilities staff initiated preliminary assessments for summer maintenance projects, including roof repairs at Franklin Middle School and HVAC system upgrades at Tendoy Elementary, with project bids expected to open in late April. The athletic department reported successful winter sports seasons and ongoing preparations for spring sports, including track and field, baseball, and softball, with practices well underway.

## Police

The Pocatello Police Department responded to 378 calls for service during the week of March 15-21. On Tuesday, March 17, officers responded to a two-vehicle collision at the intersection of Yellowstone Avenue and Alameda Road at approximately 3:45 PM. Minor injuries were reported, and traffic was diverted for nearly an hour while the scene was cleared. Driver David Thompson, 42, of Pocatello, was cited for failure to yield. On Thursday, March 19, officers investigated a report of shoplifting at the Fred Meyer store on Flandro Drive. Store security identified a suspect, later identified as Sarah Miller, 28, of Chubbuck, attempting to leave with approximately $150 worth of groceries. Miller was apprehended and charged with petty theft. Additionally, the department received 14 noise complaints, predominantly from the university area, with several resulting in warnings issued to residents.

## Fire & EMS

The Pocatello Fire Department and EMS crews responded to 125 incidents this week, including 89 medical calls and 36 fire-related incidents. On Wednesday, March 18, at approximately 1:15 AM, firefighters were dispatched to a structure fire at a residence on the 1100 block of East Wyeth Street. Engine 2 arrived to find heavy smoke visible from the single-family home. Crews quickly contained the fire to the kitchen area, preventing further spread. The cause is under investigation, but initial reports suggest it originated from a cooking accident. Damage was estimated at $35,000. No injuries were reported. On Friday, March 20, Medic 1 provided mutual aid to the Chubbuck Fire Department for a motor vehicle accident on Highway 30, assisting with patient assessment and transport.

## Real Estate

Three property transactions were recorded in Bannock County this week, reflecting continued activity in Pocatello's housing market. On March 17, a single-family home located at 1234 Elm Street, a three-bedroom, two-bathroom residence built in 1978, sold for $315,000. The property was listed by Realtor Jessica Davies of Pocatello Real Estate. On March 19, a commercial parcel at 789 North Main Street, previously home to a small retail business, changed hands for $480,000. The buyer plans to develop the site for a new professional office building. Finally, a two-bedroom townhouse at 567 Pine Avenue, near the Portneuf Greenway, sold for $245,000 on March 20. All sales were recorded at the Bannock County Recorder's Office.
