## City Government

Sanford’s City Council convened on Tuesday, March 30, reviewing several key initiatives impacting future development and city operations. Among the significant items discussed was a proposed amendment to the city’s zoning ordinance regarding accessory dwelling units. This amendment, aimed at increasing housing options, saw extensive discussion, particularly around setback requirements and owner-occupancy provisions. City Planner Mark Davis presented an overview of the changes, noting the city’s alignment with broader regional trends in housing flexibility. The Council voted to refer the draft ordinance to the Planning Board for further review and public hearing, as outlined in the March 30 City Council Agenda Packet.

Separately, the Council considered a resolution to adopt updated energy efficiency codes for municipal buildings and new construction. City Manager Sarah Chen highlighted the potential long-term cost savings and environmental benefits, projecting a 15% reduction in municipal energy consumption over five years with an initial investment of $250,000 for upgrades to the City Hall and Public Works facility. The Finance Committee, which met on March 29, had previously recommended approval of the initial funding allocation (March 29 Finance Committee Minutes). The Council approved the resolution, directing staff to develop a phased implementation plan.

In other business, the Planning Board held a workshop on March 28 to refine proposed amendments to the city’s marijuana regulation ordinance. The changes primarily focused on licensing renewals and expanded buffer zones for cultivation facilities near residential areas, in response to community feedback. Director of Economic Development, John Miller, presented data on the economic impact of existing cannabis businesses in the city (March 28 Planning Board Workshop Summary). The Conservation Commission also met on March 27, discussing preliminary plans for trail maintenance in the McKeon Reserve and reviewing a grant application for invasive species removal along the Mousam River.

## Schools

The Sanford School Board met on Thursday, March 27, to finalize its budget proposal for the upcoming fiscal year, following a series of workshops. Superintendent Dr. Evelyn Reed presented the revised $52 million budget, which includes funding for two new teaching positions at Sanford High School and an increase in athletic program stipends. The board deliberated on capital improvement requests, specifically a $1.5 million allocation for roof repairs at Willard School. After a thorough review, the board voted to approve the budget, forwarding it to the City Council for their consideration (March 27 School Board Minutes).

## Police

Sanford Police Department responded to 195 calls for service during the week. On Friday, March 28, officers responded to a report of shoplifting at a retail store on Main Street, resulting in the arrest of a 34-year-old male resident for theft. Later that day, police investigated a two-vehicle collision at the intersection of Cottage Street and High Street, which resulted in minor injuries to one driver. A noise complaint was addressed on Sunday, March 30, at a residence on Winter Street, with officers issuing a warning for excessive noise after 10 p.m.

## Fire & EMS

Sanford Fire Department and Emergency Medical Services personnel responded to 62 calls for assistance this past week. Of these, 45 were for emergency medical services. On Saturday, March 29, crews responded to a structure fire on Deering Street. Firefighters quickly contained the blaze to the kitchen area of a single-family home, preventing further spread. No injuries were reported, and the cause is under investigation. Additionally, Sanford EMS provided mutual aid to the town of North Berwick for a medical emergency on Tuesday, March 31.

## Real Estate

Several property transactions were recorded this week at the York County Registry of Deeds. A three-bedroom, two-bathroom home located at 14 Elmwood Avenue sold for $315,000. Additionally, a commercial lot spanning 2.5 acres on Airport Road, zoned for light industrial use, was purchased by Northern Logistics LLC for $1.1 million. A two-unit rental property at 8 Oak Street changed hands for $380,000, and a single-family residence at 27 River Street in Springvale sold for $295,000.

## Permits

The City of Sanford issued 7 new building permits this week. Key permits included:

-   New single-family home construction at 12 Spruce Street, estimated value $360,000.
-   Commercial interior renovation for a new restaurant at 225 Main Street, estimated value $150,000.
-   Residential addition for a sunroom at 35 Maplewood Drive, estimated value $45,000.
-   Installation of a new ground-mounted solar array at 18 Industrial Park Road, estimated value $75,000.
-   Replacement of a commercial sign for Sanford Hardware on Lafayette Street, estimated value $8,000.
-   Deck construction permit for a residence at 5 Water Street, estimated value $12,000.
-   Permit for a new detached garage at 9 Pleasant Street, estimated value $30,000.
