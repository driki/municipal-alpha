The Pocatello City Council adopted an updated floodplain management ordinance at its Thursday meeting, bringing the city into compliance with new FEMA mapping for the Portneuf River corridor. The revised ordinance expands the regulated floodplain area by approximately 120 acres and increases the required freeboard for new construction from one foot to two feet above the base flood elevation. The council voted 5-1 after a brief discussion. Council Member Scott Phillips, the sole dissenting vote, said he supports the safety requirements but is concerned about the impact on property owners who are newly within the floodplain and may now need flood insurance.

City Attorney Sam Albiston said FEMA's updated maps become effective April 15 and that the city risks losing eligibility for the National Flood Insurance Program if the ordinance is not in place by that date. Roughly 350 properties are affected by the expanded boundary.

The Airport Commission approved an amendment to hangar lease terms at its Wednesday meeting. The amendment extends the maximum lease term for privately built hangars from 20 to 30 years, matching terms offered at competing regional airports in Idaho Falls and Twin Falls. Airport Manager Dave Gibbons said the change is intended to encourage private hangar construction on the airport's undeveloped east ramp. Two hangar projects are in preliminary planning, representing an estimated $1.4 million in private investment.

The Environmental Fairness Committee heard an update Tuesday on the FMC Corporation Superfund site remediation. The EPA's contractor, Tetra Tech, reported that groundwater monitoring wells along the southern plume boundary continue to show declining phosphorus concentrations, consistent with the site's containment timeline. Committee Chair Maria Ochoa asked about the long-term monitoring schedule. The EPA's project manager said quarterly sampling will continue through at least 2030.

The Child Care Advisory Committee discussed a proposal to partner with Idaho State University on an employer-supported child care pilot. The program would subsidize 20 child care slots for city employees at participating providers. The committee asked staff to research how peer communities have funded similar programs and to present options at the April meeting.

The Parks and Recreation Commission reviewed plans for improvements at Caldwell Park, including a new playground structure, restroom renovation, and accessible path from the parking area to the pavilion. The estimated cost is $420,000. The commission recommended the project for inclusion in the FY2027 capital budget.
