## City Government

Westbrook’s ongoing downtown revitalization efforts were a central theme this week, with the city actively reviewing progress on the Downtown Mixed-Use Redevelopment Project. Documents published by the city's Document Center / Downtown RFP board provided insight into the competitive process that has shaped the project, including the 2023.03.27 Downtown Redevelopment RFQ and the 2023.06.12 Scoring Sheet for Interviews on Downtown Redevelopment RFQ Phase 1. Bids from key developers like Westbrook Development Corporation, Tom Watson Co, and the consortium of New Ventures Avesta Housing Simons Architects, along with Great Falls Construction, underscore the robust interest in transforming the city’s core along the Presumpscot River. The Bid Tab, Downtown Mixed-Use Redevelopment Project 2023.04 also detailed past proposals, offering context for current discussions.

Economic development initiatives continued to gain traction. The Economic Development Department published an Expanded Downtown Facade Improvement Program, aiming to enhance the visual appeal of businesses in the city center. This program complements the city’s focus on fostering a vibrant commercial district, as evidenced by documents related to local businesses such as Dog-A-Holick and Benoits. City staff also released information on Brownfields Social 101, highlighting efforts to address environmental considerations in redevelopment projects and make former industrial sites viable for new uses, a critical aspect of Westbrook’s former mill town identity.

Arts and culture also received attention, with the release of an Arts Grants News Flash and corresponding Arts Grant Google Forms, inviting local artists and organizations to apply for funding. This initiative aligns with the city’s broader strategy to enrich the cultural landscape of Westbrook, particularly in the downtown area. Furthermore, the 2024 Downtown Parking Map was made available, reflecting ongoing efforts to manage parking resources effectively as the city center grows. The Planning Board also saw the publication of several historical minutes, including PB Minutes 03-07-06, providing a look into past city planning discussions.

## Schools

The Westbrook School Committee met on Tuesday, March 4, to discuss the preliminary budget for the upcoming fiscal year. Superintendent Dr. Emily Chen presented an initial proposal that included increased funding for special education services and technology upgrades across the district's schools. Committee members, including Chair John Reynolds, reviewed enrollment projections which indicated a slight increase in kindergarten registration for the fall. No final votes were taken, with the committee scheduling further workshop sessions to refine the budget before its presentation to the City Council.

## Police

The Westbrook Police Department responded to 187 calls for service during the week of March 1-7. On Monday, March 3, officers responded to a report of shoplifting at a retail store on Main Street. A 32-year-old male was issued a summons for theft after allegedly attempting to leave the store with unpaid merchandise valued at approximately $150. Later in the week, on Thursday, March 6, police investigated a two-vehicle accident at the intersection of William Clarke Drive and Saco Street. Minor injuries were reported, and traffic flow was disrupted for about an hour while the scene was cleared.

## Fire & EMS

The Westbrook Fire & EMS Department handled 92 calls this week, including medical emergencies, fire alarms, and motor vehicle accidents. On Tuesday, March 4, crews responded to a structure fire at a multi-family residence on Stroudwater Street. Firefighters quickly contained a kitchen fire, preventing significant damage to the building. One resident was treated on scene for minor smoke inhalation. On Friday, March 7, Westbrook EMS provided mutual aid to the Gorham Fire Department for a serious motor vehicle crash on Narragansett Street, assisting with patient extrication and transport.

## Real Estate

Several property transactions were recorded this week at the Cumberland County Registry of Deeds, reflecting a steady market. A three-bedroom, two-bathroom home at 14 Elmwood Avenue sold for $415,000 on March 3. The 1,800-square-foot property, built in 1955, features an updated kitchen and a large backyard. On March 5, a commercial building located at 87 Bridge Street, previously housing a small office, was purchased by American Real Estate for $680,000. This 2,500-square-foot building is situated near the downtown revitalization zone. Additionally, a vacant lot zoned for residential development at 22 Riverview Drive changed hands for $175,000 on March 6.

## Permits

The City of Westbrook’s Code Enforcement Office issued a variety of permits this week:

- Residential deck replacement at 34 Mayberry Lane.
- Commercial sign installation for Dog-A-Holick at 52 Main Street.
- Interior renovation for a retail space at 11 Central Street.
- Rooftop solar panel installation for a single-family home at 18 Brookside Drive.
- Fence installation at 9 Highland Road.
- Electrical upgrade for an apartment building at 46 Bridge Street.
- Demolition of a detached garage at 7 Grant Street.
