<div class="cs-window">
<h3>What rating agencies see vs. what the documents show</h3>
<p>Rating agencies evaluate municipal credit from annual financial statements that are <span class="cs-highlight">6-18 months stale</span> by the time they're published. The governance signals that precede fiscal distress, the conversations happening in Finance Committee meetings, the RFPs for outside help, the leadership departures, are visible in public meeting minutes months or years before the financials reflect the damage.</p>
<p>A water rate hearing is legally required. <span class="cs-highlight">A finance director resigning is not.</span> The first is routine. The second is a signal.</p>
</div>

<h3>What the model detected</h3>
<p>Four governance stress categories clustered within a 12-month window:</p>
<p>
<span class="signal-cat high">Finance director departure</span>
<span class="signal-cat high">Outside financial consultant</span>
<span class="signal-cat high">Fiscal crisis language</span>
<span class="signal-cat">Audit findings</span>
</p>
<p>OPEB and pension liability discussions co-occurred with distress language in the same documents, acting as amplifiers on the governance signals.</p>
<p>Each signal individually is mundane. A city hires a consultant. An officer departs. An audit has findings. Together, clustering in the same 12-month window, they form a pattern. <span class="model-stat">Score: 62.4</span> against a distress threshold of 28.3.</p>

<div class="cs-window">
<h3>The gap nobody is filling</h3>
<p>Rating agencies only cover municipalities with outstanding rated debt. Most towns under 50,000 population never get a Moody's opinion. When they do, it's based on annual financials that are already stale.</p>
<p>State comptroller fiscal stress scores exist in a few states but rely on the same annual financials. Bloomberg terminals show what the market thinks, not what's happening on the ground.</p>
<p><span class="cs-highlight">Nobody is systematically reading the meeting minutes.</span> The Finance Committee discussion where someone says "we need to hire a financial consultant" happens 5-13 months before the CAFR reflects the problem, and 12-24 months before a rating agency acts on it.</p>
</div>
