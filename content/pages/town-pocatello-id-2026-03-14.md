The Pocatello Public Works Department presented the results of the city's lead service line inventory to the City Council Thursday. The survey, required under the EPA's Lead and Copper Rule Improvements, identified 340 service lines of unknown material out of approximately 14,000 total connections. Of those, an estimated 85 to 120 are likely lead based on construction era and neighborhood. Public Works Director Hannah Seevers said the city will prioritize sampling in the Warehouse District and Old Town neighborhoods, where most pre-1950 housing is concentrated.

The city must complete its full inventory by October 2027. Seevers estimated the cost of testing and replacement at $1.2 to $1.8 million, depending on the number of confirmed lead lines. She said staff are preparing a State Revolving Fund application.

The City Council debated a proposed fee schedule update for the Ross Park Aquatic Complex and Mountain View Event Center at Monday's meeting. The Parks and Recreation Department recommended increases of $1 to $3 per visit for pool admission and 15% for event center rentals, citing rising maintenance costs and the need to fund a new HVAC system at the event center. Council Member Linda Leeuwrik argued that the pool serves as a safety resource for children in the summer and that fee increases could reduce access for lower-income families. The council asked staff to model a tiered pricing structure and return with a revised proposal.

The Housing Alliance met Wednesday to review Community Development Block Grant allocation priorities for the 2027 program year. The committee recommended directing 40% of the estimated $380,000 allocation to housing rehabilitation, 30% to public infrastructure in low-and-moderate-income census tracts, and 30% to public services. The committee noted increased demand for the city's emergency home repair program, which has a six-month waiting list.

The Library Board received the quarterly circulation report. Digital checkouts continue to grow, up 18% year over year, while physical circulation is flat. Board member Karen Yoshida asked about expanding the library's Wi-Fi hotspot lending program, which currently has 25 devices with a waiting list of 40 patrons. Staff said they are seeking grant funding to purchase additional units.
