*Geneva, Illinois (pop. 22,000) -- Kane County. Week of March 1-7, 2026.*

The Geneva City Council voted Monday to amend the city's liquor control ordinance, creating a new "Class Z" license category. The amendment was prompted by a proposed pickleball and entertainment venue on Randall Road that doesn't fit existing license classifications. The new class allows liquor sales in recreational facilities with minimum square footage requirements and food service. The vote was 8-1, with Alderman Dean Kilburg dissenting over concerns about proximity to residential neighborhoods.

The Plan Commission approved a site plan Wednesday for a 28,000-square-foot retail center at the corner of Randall Road and Fabyan Parkway. The developer, Inland Real Estate Group, plans to anchor the center with a specialty grocer and include four smaller tenant spaces. Traffic access will be from a shared driveway with the adjacent Meijer store. The commission added a condition requiring a pedestrian connection to the existing sidewalk network.

The Public Works Committee reviewed the city's five-year capital improvement plan Tuesday. The plan totals $42 million across water, sewer, streets, and stormwater categories. The largest single item is a $6.8 million water main replacement on Batavia Avenue, scheduled for 2028. Committee Chair Don Conway asked staff to prioritize projects by asset condition rather than geographic equity, noting that some infrastructure in the older parts of the city is approaching end of life.

The Geneva Library Board discussed the feasibility of adding a makerspace to the library's lower level. Board President Karen Paulus said the library received a $50,000 matching grant from the Illinois State Library for equipment purchases. Staff will present a design proposal at the April meeting.

The Government Services Committee heard a report on the city's contract with the Kane County Animal Control facility. The current intergovernmental agreement expires in September, and the county has proposed a 12% fee increase. The committee asked staff to survey alternative providers and report back.

## Permits Issued

Five building permits were issued this week:

- A commercial interior buildout at 1901 Kaneville Road
- New single-family construction at 715 South Street
- A residential bathroom renovation at 400 East State Street
- A fence permit at 1120 South Batavia Avenue
- A patio and hardscape permit at 203 North Third Street
