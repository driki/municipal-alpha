## City Government

Portland city officials released the extensive 2027 Annual Capital Improvement Program this week, outlining planned infrastructure investments across the city (2027 Annual Capital Improvement Program). The program details projects ranging from road and bridge repairs to upgrades for public facilities and parks, with a significant focus on improving pedestrian and bicycle infrastructure in the Bayside and East Deering neighborhoods. City Manager John Smith highlighted the program's emphasis on climate resilience initiatives, including stormwater management system improvements along the waterfront and in low-lying areas, a response to recent coastal storm impacts. The Capital Improvement Programs board, an active entity this period, will oversee the phased implementation of these projects.

In other municipal news, the city received a $76,000 federal grant from the Department of Health and Human Services (Federal Grant: Department of Health and Human Services → Portland ($76K)). This funding is earmarked for expanding outreach services for unhoused youth and families, a critical need given the ongoing affordable housing challenges in Maine's largest city. Director of Health and Human Services, Sarah Chen, stated the funds would bolster existing programs at the city's family shelter and support new initiatives to connect vulnerable populations with essential resources.

A notable volume of federal filings for new tower construction emerged this week, with Robert Bridges submitting sixteen separate FAA Tower Filings (e.g., FAA Tower Filing 2026-ANM-779-NRA, FAA Tower Filing 2026-ANM-775-NRA). Each filing specifies an antenna structure height of 55 feet above ground level and appears to be part of a broader network expansion, likely for advanced telecommunications services across the city and surrounding areas. The consistent height and applicant suggest a coordinated effort to enhance cellular and data coverage, potentially impacting various neighborhoods and the city's commercial districts. The FAA OE/AAA board is actively processing these applications.

## Schools

The Portland School Board convened for a workshop session on March 25 to discuss preliminary budget projections for the upcoming fiscal year. Superintendent Lisa Rodriguez presented an overview of anticipated state subsidies and local revenue, highlighting potential challenges related to rising operational costs and a slight increase in student enrollment projections for elementary schools. The board deliberated potential investments in new STEM curriculum materials for middle schools and discussed facility needs for aging school buildings, particularly a proposed roof replacement project at Deering High School. No formal votes were taken, with further budget discussions scheduled for next month.

## Police

The Portland Police Department responded to approximately 265 calls for service during the week of March 20-26. On March 22, officers responded to a report of shoplifting at a commercial establishment on Commercial Street, resulting in the apprehension of a 34-year-old male suspect and the recovery of stolen merchandise valued at over $300. Later in the week, on March 24, police investigated a two-vehicle collision with minor injuries at the intersection of Congress Street and St. John Street, which temporarily disrupted traffic flow near the Portland Transportation Center. Additionally, officers addressed several noise complaints in the Old Port district on the evening of March 23, issuing warnings to multiple establishments.

## Fire & EMS

Portland Fire Department and EMS units responded to an estimated 140 calls this week, including medical emergencies, fire alarms, and motor vehicle accidents. On March 21, crews responded to a structure fire on Munjoy Hill. Engine 1, Ladder 1, and Rescue 1 arrived at a multi-unit dwelling on North Street to find smoke emanating from a second-story apartment. Firefighters quickly contained and extinguished a kitchen fire, preventing its spread to adjacent units. One occupant was treated on scene for smoke inhalation by EMS personnel. On March 25, a medical rescue unit provided mutual aid to the town of Cape Elizabeth for a serious motor vehicle accident on Route 77, transporting one patient to Maine Medical Center with critical injuries.

## Real Estate

Several property transactions were recorded at the Cumberland County Registry of Deeds this week:

- A three-bedroom, two-bathroom single-family home at 12 Elmwood Street in the Rosemont neighborhood sold for $585,000 on March 21.
