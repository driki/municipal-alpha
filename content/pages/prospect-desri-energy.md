## See municipal siting and zoning decisions that shape where energy projects get built

The earliest signals in energy project development come from municipal
government. A planning board that approves a solar overlay district, a
zoning board that grants a special permit for battery storage, a town
council that adopts a climate action plan — these decisions determine
where projects can be sited, what the permitting path looks like, and
whether local opposition is likely.

These documents live inside municipal CMS platforms at URLs with no
public navigation link. A notice of decision gets uploaded to a portal
the day it's issued, but nothing on the site links to it. We built
connectors that reach into each platform's document store directly,
across 15+ CMS vendors, and read them the day they're published.
Across 2,100+ municipalities in 50 states.

## How It Works

Every day, our pipeline reads new documents from municipal portals across 50 states. We classify each document by type, extract entities and financial amounts, and cross-reference against federal data (FCC, FAA, SAM.gov, FERC, EPA). The result is a structured, searchable dataset of municipal activity that updates daily.

For engineering and infrastructure firms, this means early visibility into where municipalities are investing, what they're planning, and when the RFP is coming.
