*Falmouth, Maine (pop. 12,000) -- Cumberland County. Week of March 8-14, 2026.*

---

The Falmouth Zoning Board of Appeals granted a dimensional variance Thursday for a mixed-use development project on Route 1. The applicant, Bateman Partners, sought relief from the town's setback requirements to accommodate ground-floor retail with residential units above. The board approved the request 4-1 after a public hearing that drew comments from neighboring property owners.

The School Board voted Tuesday to approve a $2.1 million facilities improvement plan covering roof replacement at Falmouth Elementary and HVAC upgrades at the middle school. Superintendent Jessica Berry said the work is expected to begin this summer, with completion before the start of the 2026-2027 school year. The board authorized staff to begin the RFP process for contractors.

The Planning Board tabled a discussion on proposed amendments to the town's telecommunications tower height regulations. The amendment, requested by a wireless carrier seeking to increase the maximum allowable height from 150 to 190 feet for an existing structure on Leighton Road, will return to the board's April agenda with additional visual impact analysis.

The Long Range Planning Advisory Committee met Monday to continue work on the comprehensive plan update, focusing on the housing chapter. Committee members reviewed survey results showing strong support for allowing accessory dwelling units in residential zones, consistent with Maine's LD 2003 mandate. The committee plans to present a draft recommendation to the Town Council in May.

The Finance Committee reviewed the town's mid-year budget report, noting that revenue from excise taxes and building permit fees is running approximately 4% ahead of projections. The committee recommended no adjustments at this time.

## Permits Issued

Seven building permits were issued this week:

- A new single-family home at 31 Foreside Road
- A detached garage at 8 Falmouth Road
- A commercial interior renovation at 240 US Route 1
- Two residential roofing replacements
- A deck addition at 15 Pleasant Hill Road
- An accessory dwelling unit at 44 Woods Road (one of the first under the town's updated ADU ordinance)
