Municipal Alpha is opening a first-of-its-kind integration: a Model Context Protocol (MCP) connector that lets you query Municipal Alpha's municipal precursor-signal corpus directly from your own AI assistant. Ask a question in Claude, or any MCP-capable client, and Municipal Alpha answers with source-cited, dated signals pulled straight from local government records.

There is no new dashboard to learn and no export to wrangle. The intelligence shows up where you already work, inside your own AI.

## What is behind it

Records from 3,400+ municipalities, 700,000+ public records, and 200,000+ classified signals, across all 50 states.

The corpus spans the documents local governments actually produce: meeting minutes and agendas, where the discussion and the draft first appear, building permits (44,000+ records), ordinances and resolutions, municipal budgets, audits and financial reports, RFPs and procurement notices, and specialized layers including cell-tower and FAA filings and vendor payment records. Municipal Alpha connects these across thousands of municipalities and reads them for the signal upstream of the decision.

## Why it matters

The edge is upstream of the decision. The discussion, the draft ordinance, the early agenda item surface months before the permit, the vote, or the RFP. Municipal Alpha captures that signal across thousands of U.S. municipalities, and now delivers it through the interface your team already uses.

## What it looks like in your AI

**Ask:** "What solar and energy activity is surfacing in Illinois?"
**Municipal Alpha returns** (Plano, IL, Plan Commission and Zoning Board of Appeals, June 1 2026): "The Plano Plan Commission and Zoning Board of Appeals will review a significant solar energy project, Plano Skies Energy Center, LLC. The agenda includes public hearings for rezoning, annexation, and a comprehensive review of the project's application." A solar development at the rezoning-hearing stage, before it becomes a permit.

**Ask:** "Where is utility and renewable infrastructure moving in New Hampshire?"
**Municipal Alpha returns** (Bow, NH, Conservation Commission, May 18 2026): "Wetland Conditional Use Permits including utility work by Eversource Energy and a solar array installation by Kearsarge Bow LF LLC." Named developers, at the environmental-permit stage.

**Ask:** "Which municipalities are planning renewable projects in New York?"
**Municipal Alpha returns** (Saratoga Springs, NY, Climate Smart Task Force, May 20 2026): "A comprehensive climate action plan focused on solar arrays and EV charging infrastructure, with energy audits by consultants like Arcadis and grant discussions." Early-stage planning, before procurement.

Every answer carries the municipality, the board, the date, and a path back to the source document.
