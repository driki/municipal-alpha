## Town Government

The Brunswick Town Council convened on Thursday, March 20, for its regular bi-weekly meeting, addressing several key municipal initiatives. Councilors approved a resolution to apply for a $1.2 million federal grant for upgrades to the town's wastewater treatment plant, a project championed by Town Manager Sarah Jenkins. The improvements are critical for maintaining compliance with environmental regulations and accommodating anticipated growth, particularly in the Brunswick Landing area. The vote was 6-1, with Councilor Emily Carter casting the dissenting vote, citing concerns over the town's long-term debt burden (Town Council Agenda, March 20, 2026).

Earlier in the week, the Planning Board met on Tuesday, March 25, to review a conditional use permit application for a proposed mixed-use development at 147 Pleasant Street. The project, submitted by Coastal Development Group, includes 30 residential units and ground-floor commercial space. Planning Director Michael Green presented the staff report, recommending approval with conditions related to traffic mitigation and open space requirements. After extensive public comment, the board voted unanimously to approve the permit, contingent on final engineering plan review by the Public Works Department (Planning Board Meeting Minutes, March 25, 2026).

Additionally, the Town Council held a workshop session on March 24 to begin preliminary discussions on the fiscal year 2027 municipal budget. Department heads presented their initial requests, with public safety and public works departments highlighting needs for equipment replacement and increased staffing. The workshop served as the first step in a multi-week budget review process, with further sessions scheduled for April. No votes were taken during this preliminary discussion.

## Schools

The Brunswick School Board held its regular meeting on Wednesday, March 26, focusing on the upcoming academic year's budget and facility maintenance projects. Superintendent Jane Doe presented an overview of the proposed fiscal year 2027 budget, which includes a 3.5% increase over the current year, primarily driven by rising healthcare costs and contractual salary obligations for teachers. The board discussed a plan to address deferred maintenance at Brunswick High School, specifically roof repairs estimated at $450,000. A public hearing on the school budget is scheduled for April 9.

## Police

Brunswick Police Department reported a total of 185 calls for service during the week of March 20-26. Officers responded to a two-vehicle collision on Bath Road near the entrance to Brunswick Landing on March 21, resulting in minor injuries to one driver and significant traffic delays for approximately an hour. No charges were filed. On March 23, officers investigated a report of shoplifting at Hannaford Supermarket on Town Hall Place, leading to the arrest of a 34-year-old local resident for theft. Police also responded to a noise complaint from residents near Bowdoin College on March 25, following reports of a loud party. Officers issued a warning to the property owner, and the gathering was dispersed without further incident.

## Fire & EMS

The Brunswick Fire Department and Emergency Medical Services responded to 62 calls for service this past week. On March 22, crews responded to a single-story structure fire at a residence on Jordan Avenue. The fire, believed to have started in the kitchen, was quickly contained, limiting damage to one room. One occupant was treated on scene for minor smoke inhalation. Brunswick also provided mutual aid to Topsham Fire Department on March 24 for a commercial building alarm activation on Main Street in Topsham, which was determined to be a false alarm.
