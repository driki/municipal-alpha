*Geneva, Illinois (pop. 22,000) -- Kane County. Week of March 15-21, 2026.*

---

The Geneva City Council approved a $1.8 million streetscape improvement project for Third Street at its Monday meeting. The project covers sidewalk replacement, decorative lighting, and new street trees from the Fox River bridge to the Metra station. Christopher B. Burke Engineering prepared the design. The council awarded the construction contract to Copenhaver Construction, with work expected to begin in May and finish before the fall festival season.

Alderman Craig Maladra asked whether the project accounts for future utility work. Public Works Director Rich Nagel said ComEd and Nicor Gas both confirmed their lines along the corridor are in serviceable condition and not scheduled for replacement. The council approved the project unanimously.

The Plan Commission held a public hearing Wednesday on a proposed four-story mixed-use development at 14 South Third Street. The developer, Lexington Homes, presented plans for 36 residential units above ground-floor retail space. Several residents from adjacent properties raised concerns about parking and building height relative to the neighboring Victorian homes. The commission continued the hearing to its April meeting and requested a shadow study.

The Finance Committee met Tuesday to review the city's pension funding status. City Administrator Stephanie Dawkins reported that the police pension fund is 62% funded and the fire pension fund is 58% funded, both below the state's target of 90% by 2040. The committee discussed options including increasing the annual levy contribution and exploring a pension obligation bond. No action was taken.

The Natural Resources Committee received an update on the Fox River water quality monitoring program. Recent testing showed phosphorus levels slightly above the Illinois EPA's recommended threshold at the Route 38 sampling point. The committee asked staff to coordinate with the Fox River Study Group and report back in May.
