## See where municipalities are opening the door to development before the RFP drops

The earliest signals in real estate development come from municipal
government, not from brokers or listing services. A comprehensive plan
update that adds "mixed-use corridor" language, a planning board that
designates a redevelopment area, a town council that approves a new
TIF district, these actions reshape what can be built, where, and at
what cost, months or years before a shovel hits dirt.

These documents live inside municipal CMS platforms at URLs with no
public navigation link. A zoning amendment gets uploaded to a portal
and nothing on the site points to it. We built connectors that reach
into each platform's document store directly, across 15+ CMS vendors,
and read them the day they're published. Across 2,100+ municipalities
in 50 states.

## How It Works

Every day, our pipeline reads new documents from municipal portals across 50 states. We classify each document by type, extract entities and financial amounts, and cross-reference against federal data (FCC, FAA, SAM.gov, FERC, EPA). The result is a structured, searchable dataset of municipal activity that updates daily.

For engineering and infrastructure firms, this means early visibility into where municipalities are investing, what they're planning, and when the RFP is coming.
