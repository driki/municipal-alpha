Question 6 of 8 · The Martian : block or warn

The answer became a rule: not every guardrail should block
MECHANICAL · block on violation

When I cannot reliably catch it.

A fabricated bio, an em-dash in buyer copy, a schema typo that silently drops data. Fail closed.

JUDGMENT · surface and defer

When pushing through is the point.

Scope creep, a rat-hole. Surface the question once, then let me decide. Push-through is where the good stuff lives.

“founders need guardrails, and pushback, but when they push through those pushbacks, sometimes you get the novel stuff”

Speaker · 4 min · most distinctive idea · LAND THIS

  • I kept asking the system to stop me doing the thing I know I do, build instead of ship
  • The room-prompt is a real debate, ask them where their line is
  • My answer turned into the most useful rule in here, there are two kinds of guardrail
  • A mechanical rule blocks on violation, because I can't reliably catch the thing, a fabricated bio, an em-dash in copy, a schema typo that silently eats data, fail closed
