Question 1 of 4

How early can I see a tower lease deal coming in a region I cover?

Tower operators need to identify under-signaled markets before the RFP queue. Municipal records carry the signal 24+ months before any federal record exists.

How to ask it
Find me parcels in [region] with municipal action in the last 24 months suggesting tower-ready land, annexations, lease language, communications-infrastructure siting at police/fire properties, citizen complaints about cell coverage.

This question is asked by tower REITs and aggregators (American Tower, SBA, Crown Castle, mid-size aggregators) and the PE firms that own them. It's also asked by carriers evaluating co-location opportunities. The buyer wants to identify deal flow before it hits the formal RFP, that's where the lease economics are best and where competitive bidding hasn't started yet.

The corpus tracks every municipal mention through a 9-stage process model, citizen input, workshop, committee, public hearing, budget appropriation, formal vote, RFP prep, RFP issued, award, with a confidence score at each stage that reflects institutional momentum. The buyer gets the chain, not just the final award. The example below traces one Amherst NH tower from a single July 2023 citizen complaint to a January 2026 unanimous vote.

Answer view, data from the corpus

Amherst, NH, 32-month signal chain, July 2023 to March 2026. A single citizen complaint about cell coverage in the Village section of Amherst triggered a chain of 17 municipal-record signals across 15 meetings, ending with a unanimous Board of Selectmen vote authorizing a 30-year ground lease with American Tower Company. No FCC Antenna Structure Registration exists. No FAA filing exists. The only record of this tower is in municipal meeting minutes.

Below: the chain, with the corpus's 9-stage confidence score at each step. Confidence rose from 0.12 (citizen input) to 1.00 (final award), with a documented dip at the design-pivot moment when a combined cell/communications-tower concept proved non-viable.

No federal record exists. 23-month competitive window from first vendor engagement (Feb 2024) to American Tower named (Jan 2026), that's the window where a tower operator with the corpus would have seen this deal forming and could have engaged.
