What the record holds

Economic development is one slice of it, not the whole thing

The municipal record is wider than any single use case. Between the two of you the work spans long-range planning, housing, infrastructure, broadband, fiscal health, and workforce, and all of it leaves a paper trail in the same agendas and minutes we already read. Different seats care about different slices.

economic development housing & zoning infrastructure & broadband fiscal health & budgets long-range & comprehensive planning workforce & grants transportation & PACTS

The point of the shared engine is that you do not have to pick the angle up front. The same read can be pointed at whichever of these is the real pressure for your region this year, and re-pointed as that changes. The individual pages I sent each of you take the economic-development cut as one worked example; the conversation is open to any of the others.

What the product is

A living read, not a static dataset or a hand-written brief

New municipal activity matching what you care about, surfaced as it posts with a source quote and link; the historical record pulled on demand for the fights and comparables; and a relevance loop where you mark what was useful and the feed sharpens to your region. You define the topics, the towns, and what counts as a signal. We keep the read current, and a town you name can be stood up quickly because the engine reads any municipality that publishes.

The agenda for Thursday

Mostly, I want to listen

You each have a region-specific page with the detail, so we do not need to spend the call on what the tool is. The point of the conversation is the other direction, understanding your world well enough to know whether and where this is useful. I am deliberately not assuming a pathway. A few open questions I will be asking:

What lands on your desk later than you would like, that you wish you had seen at the front end?

Across everything your agencies do, where would a wide, current read of the municipal record actually change a decision?

Is this most useful for your own planning and analysis, or as something you would put in front of your member towns?

And if economic development is not the first thing this is for, what is?

The fastest way to make it concrete: each of you name one town, or one topic you are tracking right now, and I will run the read and show you what the record surfaces in your own backyard.
