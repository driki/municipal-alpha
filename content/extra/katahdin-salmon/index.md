## The timeline, from Millinocket's public record

### June 24 and 26, 2025: the first mention
The Town Manager's report to the Millinocket Town Council lists a new name among the partners working on the former mill site's cleanup and redevelopment:

> "I am working with our Brownfields Team, Our Katahdin, the EPA, the MDEP, Castlerock, Katahdin Salmon to schedule an informational community meeting later this summer at which we will provide updates to cleanup work successes, next steps, and updates to these new business investments in the works."

At this point the salmon project is one line, grouped with the brownfield cleanup team. No dollar figure, no scale. Just a named partner and a promise of "new business investments in the works."

### August 13 and 14, 2025: the signal holds
The Town Manager's report repeats the same language: Katahdin Salmon, still listed with Our Katahdin, the EPA, the MDEP, and Castlerock, still pointing toward an informational community meeting and "these new business investments in the works."

### September 11, 2025: the scale becomes public
Three months after the first mention, the picture sharpens. In a section of the report titled "Provide Enhanced Facilities to Support Private Investment in the Region," the Town puts numbers on the table:

> "Great Northern Salmon is investing $300 million into developing part of the former mill site to house a salmon farm. When completed, the salmon farm is expected to produce 10,000 metric tons of salmon annually. These private investments are expected to generate numerous permanent jobs and serve as a catalyst for additional business development throughout the region."

The same report shows the salmon farm is not the only thing moving:

> "Castlerock Biofuels LLC, through its subsidiary EME Biofuels LLC, is in the process of developing a new renewable fuel oil facility at the One North industrial park in Millinocket... full-scale operations expected to be up and running by 2027. This is expected to add approximately 80 full-time jobs once operational, according to Our Katahdin."

And it credits the local development engine by name, noting "the attached letter of support from Our Katahdin."
