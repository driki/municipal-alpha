The timeline, from Millinocket's public record

Three reports to the Millinocket Town Council, across the summer of 2025. Watch the signal go from one line to a nine-figure number.

  • June 24 & 26, 2025 · Town Manager's Report
    First mention, as "Katahdin Salmon," listed among the brownfield cleanup partners (Our Katahdin, the EPA, the MDEP, Castlerock) and an upcoming community meeting on "new business investments in the works." One line. No numbers yet.
    Source ▸ Town Manager's Report, Jun 26 2025
  • August 13 & 14, 2025 · Town Manager's Report
    The same language repeats. Katahdin Salmon, still grouped with Our Katahdin and the cleanup team, still pointing toward the community meeting and "these new business investments in the works." The signal holds.
    Source ▸ Council agenda, Aug 14 2025
  • September 11, 2025 · Council agenda
    The scale becomes public. Under a heading titled "Provide Enhanced Facilities to Support Private Investment in the Region," the town names Great Northern Salmon investing $300 million in a salmon farm on the former mill site, projected at 10,000 metric tons a year, "a catalyst for additional business development throughout the region."
    Source ▸ Council agenda, Sep 11 2025

From a name in a cleanup partner list to a $300 million figure, the whole arc is visible in the public record, months before any permit or RFP.

The receipts

The exact words, from the town's own documents.

  • The first mention

    "I am working with our Brownfields Team, Our Katahdin, the EPA, the MDEP, Castlerock, Katahdin Salmon to schedule an informational community meeting… updates to… these new business investments in the works."

    Town Manager's Report · Jun 26, 2025
  • The scale

    "Great Northern Salmon is investing $300 million into developing part of the former mill site to house a salmon farm. When completed, the salmon farm is expected to produce 10,000 metric tons of salmon annually… a catalyst for additional business development throughout the region."

    Council agenda · Sep 11, 2025
  • Not the only thing moving

    "Castlerock Biofuels LLC, through its subsidiary EME Biofuels LLC, is… developing a new renewable fuel oil facility at the One North industrial park… expected to add approximately 80 full-time jobs once operational, according to Our Katahdin."

    Council agenda · Sep 11, 2025
