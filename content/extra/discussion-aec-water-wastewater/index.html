Northeast geotechnical engineering principal, on getting in early

By the time a municipal water or wastewater project is a public RFP, the firms already in the conversation have the edge. A second engineering principal put the same dynamic plainly: "they're in the door before we are." The early signal (the workshop, the committee item, the capital plan) is all public, but it sits unread on hundreds of separate town websites, so most firms find out at the RFP, on the same day as everyone else, or never.

What Municipal Alpha is

Every city, town, and district publishes the decisions that move water and wastewater infrastructure before they happen: the capital improvement plan, the budget-workshop priority, the committee discussion, the engineering-services contract award. Those records land months ahead of the RFP, scattered across hundreds of separate town websites in incompatible formats where almost none reach a search engine.

Municipal Alpha reads them the night they post, classifies what matters, verifies every signal against the source document, and surfaces the project while there is still time to position around it.

Show details for the method, and the question every technical reader asks ▸

What it reads
Agendas, minutes, capital improvement plans, budget workshops, committee discussions, engineering-services and construction contract awards, the night they post.
What it produces
Classified signals, each scored for confidence and linked back to the source record, assembled across time.
The method, not AI
A deterministic navigator and classification layer over a registry of where each government's records actually live. The model labels a document once it is in hand. It is not the moat.
The question a technical reader asks
"Given the pace of AI, can't an agent just do this? Point a model at the towns and get the same thing?"
  • The corpus. The history does not exist on a search engine; it has been read and structured night after night. A model started today has no back-record.
  • The upstream work is not AI work. Finding where each town's records live, fingerprinting the platform, and extracting clean text is a data-engineering problem, not a prompting one.
  • A feed, not a query. You get the signal pushed the night it posts, not an answer when you remember to ask.
  • The specific signal, and an honest one. Ask any model which Maine towns opened a wastewater capital plan this quarter and it will invent a town, a date, a citation. Every Municipal Alpha signal links back to the source document, so you get an answer you can defend, not one you have to trust.
