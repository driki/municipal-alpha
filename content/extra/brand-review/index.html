What Municipal Alpha does

Every day, 19,500 town halls in America publish documents that predict market events weeks or months before they happen. Vendor payments that precede earnings reports. Planning board votes that precede building permits. Budget approvals that precede RFPs. The documents have always been public. They were just scattered across thousands of websites in incompatible formats, and nobody was reading them all.

Municipal Alpha reads them all, every day, across 2,300+ municipalities in all 50 states. A check register shows a vendor payment weeks before it shows up in quarterly earnings. A planning board vote signals a zoning change months before a building permit is filed. A budget approval reveals an infrastructure project before the RFP exists. The precursor documents were always there, encoded in public record, waiting for someone to read them.

The category is precursor intelligence. You see the thing before it becomes a thing.

The logo concept

Look at the wordmark below. It says "municipal." But the "a" is actually a Greek alpha (α). Most people read right past it and just see the word. Once you notice it, you can't unsee it.

That's the whole company in one typographic move. The precursor signal was always there, encoded in plain sight, obvious once you know where to look. The α does double duty: it's the "a" in municipal, and it's alpha, as in the investment term for seeing something before everyone else does. You see the thing before it becomes a thing.

The serif typeface is deliberate. Public records, newspapers, legal documents, the Congressional Record. Serif is the typography of things that are on the record. The α is set in a lighter weight than the bold letters around it, the same way the precursor signal is quieter than the noise it sits inside.
