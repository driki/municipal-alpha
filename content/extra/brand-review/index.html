What this is

Every day, 19,500 town halls publish documents that predict market events weeks before they happen. A vendor payment hits a check register three weeks before quarterly earnings. A planning board vote signals a zoning change months before a permit is filed. A budget line reveals an infrastructure project before the RFP exists.

The documents have always been public. They were just scattered across thousands of websites in incompatible formats, and nobody was reading them all. Municipal Alpha reads them all. 2,300+ municipalities, 50 states, updated daily.

The signal was in the record. We found it first.

The concept

Look at the wordmark. It says "municipal." But the "a" is a Greek alpha (α). Most people read right past it. Once you notice it, you can't unsee it.

That's the whole company in one typographic move. The signal was always there, encoded in plain sight. The α does double duty: it's the "a" in municipal, and it's alpha — the investment term for edge. Where alpha hides.
