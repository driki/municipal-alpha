Tower siting in the municipal record.

Tower siting decisions show up in town records years before they show up in FAA or FCC databases. By the time a permit is on file, the deliberation is over, the spec is written, and the lease counterparty is locked in. The earlier signal lives in select-board packets, planning-board agendas, zoning-bylaw revisions, and outside-counsel memos, and it is currently scattered across the same kind of municipal records that other intelligence stacks treat as out-of-scope.

Today's corpus carries 495 substantive tower-siting documents across 138 municipalities in 31 US states, with the earliest mention landing in 2007 and the most recent in May 2026. This report walks two depth examples, then names the cross-state shape.

Section 1 · What's in the corpus

The pre-RFP layer that does not appear in FAA or FCC feeds.

FAA and FCC databases capture tower siting after the fact: filings made when construction is committed, location agreed, lease executed. The signals that lead to those filings sit upstream, in municipal deliberation. Naming them is the precondition for the rest of the report.

Section 2 · Andover MA · the policy chain

Six years of small-cell wireless deliberation, traceable through one board.

Andover's Select Board has been working on small-cell wireless policy since 2019. The shape of that work is visible in seven board packets across 2023 to 2025, with the upstream trigger landing in 2022 and the base policy reaching back to April 2019. None of this surfaced in press; all of it surfaced in meeting packets.

2019, April 11 · base policy effective

The Town of Andover Select Board adopted the policy titled "Applications for Small Cell Wireless Installations," effective as of April 11, 2019. This is the 2019 Policy referenced in subsequent counsel memos.

Fall 2022 · Verizon Wireless grant of location proceedings

Verizon Wireless filed grant-of-location proceedings with the town. This is the procedural step under Massachusetts G.L. c.166 §§21–22 that authorizes carrier work in the public right-of-way. The Verizon filing is what triggered the next two years of board work.

2022, October 17 · first Select Board hearing

The Board took up the Cellco Partnership d/b/a Verizon Wireless application for the first time. Board questions led the town to engage outside counsel from two firms.

Pattern

One carrier filing in 2022 produced a counsel engagement in 2022, a third-reading vote in 2023, and an ongoing policy revision through 2025. Six years of traceable signal, all in Select Board packets, all preceding any new permit or RFP. Any portfolio holding tower assets or land near small-cell-eligible right-of-way in Andover had two years of lead time before the policy posture changed.

Section 5 · Buyer-shape note

The same corpus serves two different shapes of question.

Tower-siting signal in municipal records is read differently by different kinds of buyer. Naming the shape avoids sending the wrong cut to the wrong reader.

Operator / aggregator reading (depth shape)

An aggregator or tower REIT evaluating ground-lease acquisition cares about specific parcels, specific landowners, specific competing carrier filings within the same ZIP code. The Acton PBSP-25-3 file names the applicant entity (ARX Wireless), the parcel owner (MGCC Realty LLC), the address (125 Nagog Park), the application date, the decision date, and the conditions. That is the kind of depth that supports a lease underwriting decision.

This reading also surfaces deal-relevant counterparty diversity: the Acton PBSP-25-7 grant is on Acton-Boxborough Regional School District land, which is an unusual lease counterparty most aggregators would not see in standard databases until much later. The corpus surfaces it the day the decision is posted.

Systematic / quant reading (breadth shape)

A systematic investor evaluating tower-siting as a regional or thematic signal cares about cross-state cadence, year-over-year volume, and the geography of friction. The 31-state breadth, the year distribution, and the entity-type distribution above are starting cuts. With a longer history backfill and onboarding into the ~20 US states currently underrepresented, the same corpus supports panel-level analysis: number of small-cell ordinance revisions per quarter by state, number of out-of-state tower-developer applicants per region, school-district vs commercial-LLC vs private-individual lease-counterparty mix over time.

Brand intelligence reading

A corporate communications or brand intelligence team at a tower entity (Verizon Wireless, AT&T Mobility, Cellco Partnership, American Tower, Crown Castle, SBA, TowerCo, ARX Wireless) cares about where their own name appears in municipal proceedings before the conversation reaches news. The Verizon report at municipalalpha.com/verizon walks the same shape for one entity. The same method applies one-for-one to AT&T, T-Mobile, Crown Castle, American Tower, SBA, and the smaller tower-development LLCs visible in Acton.

Section 7 · Where this fits

A note on the tools already in your stack.

FAA antenna structure registrations and FCC license filings are downstream layers: by the time an entry is filed, the lease is executed and the site selected. Wireless Estimator, BeAccurate, and adjacent FCC-derived feeds work this layer. The competitive intelligence question they cannot answer is when and where the next application is forming, who is filing, and what the local policy posture is.

That is the gap this report sits in. The municipal-meeting layer carries 536,000+ documents across 31 states with new docs landing every night. The corpus already includes named tower-class entities (Cellco Partnership, Verizon Wireless, AT&T Mobility, ARX Wireless Infrastructure, TowerCo VI LLC, American Tower, Crown Castle, SBA Communications) in formal filings, with applicant, parcel, owner, hearing date, and decision in the public record.

The product shape for an aggregator or tower REIT is operational: a weekly cadence of pre-RFP signal scoped to a stated footprint, formatted for the workflow already in place. The product shape for a systematic investor is panel-level: a cleanly structured time series of tower-siting events by region, with applicant and counterparty classification, suitable for backtest.

Either shape, the corpus is built. The conversation is about pacing the next expansion to a specific buyer's footprint, on terms that work for both sides.
