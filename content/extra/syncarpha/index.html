The layer where a project lives or dies

A solar project is won or lost at the local level long before it is public: an overlay district, a utility-scale moratorium, a special-use denial at the planning board, a setback ordinance that quietly takes a parcel off the table. That layer is spread across hundreds of small jurisdictions, each with its own agenda, and almost no one aggregates it. By the time it reaches an RFP or a trade headline, the decision is already made.

I read that layer, every planning board and council and zoning agenda, across hundreds of towns, every day.

What it surfaces when a project is live

On an active site, that quiet layer is anything but. What I flag, with the source document attached:

  • A planning board adopting, or rejecting, a solar overlay district.
  • A council enacting or extending a utility-scale or ground-mount moratorium.
  • A zoning board denying a special-use permit, with the reasoning in the minutes.
  • An ordinance change to setbacks, acreage caps, or screening that takes parcels off the table.
  • The first informal appearance of a competing project on an agenda, before it is filed.

Every one carries a link to the signed document on the town's own portal, dated to the meeting it came from. Not a summary, the record.
